LinkToDish Support

Answers to common questions and how to reach us

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I import a recipe from a website?

Tap the Add tab, then choose Import from URL. Paste the recipe page URL and LinkToDish will extract the recipe automatically.

How do I create a recipe manually?

Tap the Add tab, then choose Enter Manually. Fill in the title, ingredients, steps, and any other details, then tap Save.

Can I add photos to my recipes?

Yes. When creating or editing a recipe, scroll to the Photos section. You can pick up to 3 photos from your library or take a new one with your camera (iOS).

Where is my data stored?

Your recipes are stored locally on your device. If you have iCloud enabled, they sync automatically across your iPhone, iPad, and Mac through your private iCloud account.

Can I edit a recipe after saving it?

You can edit any recipe you created manually. Open the recipe and tap the Edit button in the toolbar. Imported recipes are currently read-only.

What happens if I close the app while creating a recipe?

Your in-progress recipe is auto-saved as a draft. The next time you tap Enter Manually, you'll be asked if you want to resume your draft or start fresh. Drafts are marked in your recipe list so you can find them easily.

How do I share a recipe?

Open a recipe and use the Share button to send it via the system share sheet, copy it as formatted text, or export it as a .recipebuilder file that others can open in LinkToDish.

How do I organize recipes into categories?

Go to the Categories tab to create and manage categories. You can also assign categories to a recipe when creating or viewing it.

Does LinkToDish work on iPad and Mac?

Yes. LinkToDish is a universal app for iOS 17+, iPadOS 17+, and macOS 14+. It uses tabs on iPhone and a split view on iPad and Mac.
